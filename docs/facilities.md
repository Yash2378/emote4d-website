## Section 1 — Privacy that survives procurement review

In a regulated residential setting, the hardest question isn't "does it work" — it's
"what happens to the footage." Most camera systems answer that with a policy. EMOTE4D
answers it with architecture.

There is no footage. The camera's image is converted to a stick figure on the device
itself and the original frame is discarded. Nothing is stored, nothing is transmitted,
and there is no cloud account that can be breached, subpoenaed, or sold. That is the
difference between *promising* privacy and *not having the data in the first place* —
and it's the version your residents, their families, and your legal team can sign off on.

- No raw video stored or transmitted
- No facial recognition — the system reads posture, not identity
- Processing stays on the device, in the room

---

## Section 2 — Built against alert fatigue

The fastest way to make a fall-detection system useless is to cry wolf. When staff get
false alerts, they stop trusting all alerts — and the one that matters gets ignored.

EMOTE4D is designed around that problem. A fall has to clear two independent checks
before anyone is notified: a machine-learning model first flags the motion, then a
separate step confirms the person is actually down before an alert goes out. The goal
isn't to detect everything that *might* be a fall — it's to be worth paying attention to
when it does alert.

---

## Section 3 — No cloud, no Wi-Fi dependency for the part that matters

Fall detection runs entirely on the device and works offline. Your residents' safety
doesn't hinge on facility Wi-Fi staying up or a vendor's cloud staying online. There is
no per-room cloud subscription required for the core safety function, and no monthly
fee gating whether the system actually watches for falls.

---

## Section 4 — Designed for how care teams actually work

- **Alerts where staff already are:** a fall triggers an SMS to the responders you
  designate, with a short verification delay built in.
- **Simple to place:** one device per room, positioned to cover the space — no
  wearables for residents to remember, charge, or refuse.
- **Quiet by default:** no screens for residents, no two-way audio surveillance, no
  always-on video feed for anyone to watch.
